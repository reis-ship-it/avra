## ü§ù Business Partnership System

### **Partnership Types**

1. **User-Business Partnerships** (2-party)
   - Expert + Verified Business
   - Co-host events
   - Revenue sharing

2. **Multi-Party Sponsorships** (N-party)
   - Influencer + Venue + Sponsor(s)
   - Financial and product sponsorships
   - Pre-event agreements

3. **User-Initiated Outreach**
   - Qualified users can invite businesses through app
   - **Referral Incentives:**
     - $50 immediate bonus when business completes signup
     - 10% of business's first event earnings
     - Maximum total bonus: $200 per referral
   - **Unaffiliated Business Flow:**
     - User searches for venue
     - If not on SPOTS: "Invite to SPOTS" option appears
     - User earns referral fee for onboarding
     - Business receives invitation email with partnership proposal
   - Vibe-based matching (70%+ compatibility required for suggestions)

### **Partnership Workflow**

1. **Discovery** - AI suggests compatible partners (70%+ vibe match only)
2. **Proposal** - One party proposes partnership with revenue split
3. **Negotiation** - Revenue split discussion (can counter-propose)
4. **Agreement** - **ALL parties must approve and LOCK before event starts**
   - **CRITICAL:** Splits cannot be changed after event starts
   - Agreement locked when all parties approve
   - Event cannot go live until agreement is locked
5. **Execution** - Event happens
6. **Payment** - Automatic revenue distribution (2 days after event)
7. **Feedback** - Mutual ratings (both can rate each other)

### **Vibe-Based Matching (70%+ Compatibility Required)**

**The Rule:**
> System will ONLY suggest partnerships if the vibes for both match (70%+ compatibility). Both parties can still say no, but system won't suggest bad matches.

**How It Works:**
- Personality/vibe data analyzed for compatibility
- Only 70%+ matches shown as suggestions
- Reduces spam and mismatches
- Higher acceptance rates (68% vs ~10% without filtering)
- Both parties can still decline (but rarely need to)

**Vibe Factors Considered:**
- Value alignment
- Quality focus
- Community orientation
- Event style preferences
- Authenticity vs. commercial focus

### **Both Parties Can Decline**

**Key Principle:**
- Business and experts should have the ability to say no to a partnership
- System only suggests if vibes match (70%+)
- But either party can still decline for any reason
- No forced partnerships
- Mutual consent required

## üéì Expertise System - Complete Specification

### **Core Principle**

> **As the platform grows, becoming an "expert" should become harder to prevent oversaturation and maintain trust. Expertise should be easy at first, but require more as more people use the app.**

### **Multiple Paths to Expertise (Weighted System)**

**Path 1: Exploration (40% weight)**
- Automatic location-based check-ins (no phone required)
- Dwell time quality scoring (5 min = 0.5, 30+ min = 1.0)
- Reviews and ratings given
- Breadth (variety of spots) and depth (repeat visits)
- **Technology:** Bluetooth ai2ai proximity detection + geofencing

**Path 2: Credentials (25% weight)**
- University degrees (BA, MS, PhD)
- Professional certifications (Q Grader, Sommelier, etc.)
- Published work (articles, books, research papers)
- Industry experience (years in field)
- Awards and recognition
- **Verification:** LinkedIn, employer letters, licenses, portfolio review

**Path 3: Influence (20% weight)**
- SPOTS platform influence:
  - Followers interested in category
  - List engagement (saves, shares)
  - Community recognition
- External platform influence:
  - Instagram/TikTok/YouTube followers (verified)
  - Blog/website following
  - **Yelp Elite status** (verified Yelp Elite badge)
  - **Food bloggers** (verified food blog with following)
  - **Writers of any blog** (verified blog with expertise content)
  - **Anyone with online presence** (verified social media, blog, website, podcast, etc.)
- List curation:
  - Number of quality lists created
  - How many users saved/followed lists
- **Normalization:** Logarithmic scale (diminishing returns)
- **Easy Transition:** Yelp Elite, food bloggers, and verified online presence can transition expertise easily with verification

**Path 4: Professional (25% weight - separate from credentials)**
- **Culinary Professionals:** Chefs (head, sous, executive), baristas (professional, competition-level), sommeliers (certified), bartenders (professional, mixologists), pastry chefs, restaurant owners/operators
- **Writers & Critics:** Food writers/critics, art critics, music journalists, authors (published), editors (magazines, publications)
- **Educators:** Professors (university), teachers (K-12, specialized), instructors (professional training), coaches (certified), mentors (verified programs)
- **Consultants & Advisors:** Industry consultants, specialists (verified expertise), analysts (professional)
- **Arts & Culture:** Curators (museum, gallery), gallerists (gallery owners), museum directors, professional artists
- **Healthcare:** Doctors (licensed), nurses (RN, licensed), therapists (licensed), nutritionists (certified)
- **Proof of Work Required:** LinkedIn cross-reference, employer letter, license/certification, portfolio review, OR peer endorsements (3+)

**Path 5: Community (15% weight)**
- Questions answered (helping newcomers)
- Quality list curation
- Events successfully hosted
- Peer endorsements from other experts
- Constructive feedback given
- Community contributions (guides, tips)

**Path 6: Golden Local Expert (Special Status)**
- **Requirements:** 25+ continuous years living in one place
- **Verification:** Property deed/tax records, utility bills (25+ years), voter registration history, driver's license records, school records, community attestations (3+ residents)
- **Special Powers:**
  - Event curation and review (community fit assessment)
  - Neighborhood advisory board membership
  - Event visibility boost (1.5x relevance score)
  - Community guide designation
  - Historical context provider
  - Cultural preservation role

### **Automatic Location-Based Check-Ins**

**Technology Stack:**
- Background location detection (geofencing, 50m radius)
- Bluetooth ai2ai proximity verification (works offline)
- Dwell time calculation (5+ minutes = valid visit)
- Quality scoring (longer stay = higher quality)

**User Experience:**
- **Zero friction** - Completely automatic, no phone interaction needed
- Works with phone in pocket
- Works offline (Bluetooth-based)
- Visit logged automatically with quality score
- Optional review prompt 2 hours later

**Visit Quality Formula:**
```
Quality = f(dwell time, review given, repeat visit)
- Quick stop (5 min, no review): 0.5 points
- Normal visit (15 min, no review): 0.8 points
- Long stay (30+ min, no review): 1.0 points
- Normal + review (15 min + rating): 1.3 points
- Long + detailed review: 1.5 points
```

### **Dynamic Scaling System**

**Platform Phases:**
- **Bootstrap** (<1K users): Lower thresholds (10 visits, 5 ratings, 4.0‚òÖ)
- **Growth** (1K-10K): Moderate thresholds (20 visits, 10 ratings, 4.2‚òÖ)
- **Scale** (10K-100K): Higher thresholds (35 visits, 20 ratings, 4.3‚òÖ)
- **Mature** (100K+): Highest thresholds (50 visits, 35 ratings, 4.5‚òÖ)

**Category-Specific Multipliers:**
- Popular categories require more (saturation multiplier)
- Example: Coffee at 1.5x saturation ‚Üí requirements √ó 1.5
- Prevents oversaturation in popular categories

### **Sophisticated Saturation Algorithm (6 Factors)**

**Old Formula (Too Simple):**
```
Saturation = Experts / Total Users
If > 2%, increase requirements
```

**New Formula (Sophisticated):**
```
Saturation Score = 
  (Supply Ratio √ó 25%) +         // How many experts exist
  ((1 - Quality) √ó 20%) +        // Are they good? (inverted)
  ((1 - Utilization) √ó 20%) +    // Are they being used? (inverted)
  ((1 - Demand) √ó 15%) +         // Do users want more? (inverted)
  (Growth Instability √ó 10%) +   // Is growth healthy?
  (Geographic Clustering √ó 10%)  // Are they concentrated?
```

**Six Factors Explained:**
1. **Supply Ratio (25%):** Traditional expert count vs. users
2. **Quality Distribution (20%):** Are experts actually good? (avg rating, std dev)
3. **Utilization Rate (20%):** Are experts being used? (active experts, events hosted)
4. **Demand Signal (15%):** Do users want more? (searches, wait lists, follows)
5. **Growth Velocity (10%):** Is expert growth healthy? (stable vs. explosive)
6. **Geographic Distribution (10%):** Are experts clustered or spread? (coverage analysis)

**Result:** Nuanced recommendations (not just "increase/decrease"), considers quality, demand, utilization, and geography.

**How It Works:**

- Can host events in areas where you have City-level expertise

## üèõÔ∏è Club Pages System

### **Club Pages with Admin Hierarchy**

**The Feature:**
> Club pages with admins and internal hierarchy so the admins can allow for people in the club to host club events and spots connected places.

### **Club Structure:**

```dart
class Club {
  final String id;
  final String name;
  final String description;
  final String category;  // "Food", "Art", "Music", etc.
  final ClubAdminHierarchy hierarchy;
  final List<ClubMember> members;
  final List<String> connectedSpots;  // Spots the club is connected to
  final ClubPermissions permissions;
}

class ClubAdminHierarchy {
  final ClubAdmin owner;           // Club creator (full control)
  final List<ClubAdmin> admins;    // Can manage members and events
  final List<ClubAdmin> moderators; // Can approve events
  final List<ClubMember> members;  // Regular members
}
```
