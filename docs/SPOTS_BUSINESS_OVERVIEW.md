### **The Problem: America's Loneliness Epidemic**

In May 2023, U.S. Surgeon General Dr. Vivek Murthy declared **loneliness and social isolation a public health epidemic** in the United States, highlighting its profound threats to health and well-being. The crisis is severe and growing:

**The Scale of the Problem:**
- **57% of Americans** report feelings of loneliness (up from 46% in 2018), according to The Cigna Group's 2024 study
- **61% of young adults** (ages 13-24) say loneliness impacts their mental health, with 35% stating it disrupts their daily lives (Hopelab & Data for Progress, 2025)
- **40% of adults 45+** report being lonely, rising from 35% in previous years (AARP, 2025)
- **52 million Americans** feel lonely "a lot of the day yesterday" (Gallup, 2024)

**The Decline of Community Spaces:**
The erosion of "third places"‚Äîinformal gathering spots like coffee shops, libraries, and community centers‚Äîhas accelerated social isolation:
- **23% reduction** in grocery and convenience stores (2008-2015)
- **17% reduction** in religious organizations
- **18% reduction** in public recreation and entertainment locations

**The Health Impact:**
Chronic loneliness increases the risk of premature death by **26-32%**‚Äîcomparable to smoking 15 cigarettes daily. Poor social connection increases risk for coronary heart disease by 29%, stroke by 32%, and dementia by 50%.

**The Root Cause:**
People lack accessible, meaningful ways to discover and connect with communities in their real-world neighborhoods. Traditional platforms prioritize digital engagement over real-world connection, and existing location apps focus on transactions, not community building.
