I was surrounded by incredible options: food, coffee, places, events, all with different but equally good vibes. But I couldn't decide. I wanted something to help me figure out where to go, something that could learn my vibe and give me suggestions‚Äînot just an easily forgettable list saved in Google Maps.

The only way I could think of making that work was by making the system learn my vibe and the vibe of what's around me, so it could match up perfectly. But I realized something profound: vibes change constantly, from second to second. They are fleeting moments, constantly shifting.

That was when I realized I needed to create a system that was always learning the vibe of what's around me through the people around me‚Äîa system reflective of the world around us.

**The AI2AI system was born from a simple thought:** People subconsciously change in various micro ways just by being near people. An AI agent that reflects that can help us narrow in on our vibe because now those subconscious behaviors can talk to each other, have conversations about mutual likes and dislikes (without disagreeing), and can learn to improve our lives without us having to even think about it.

I dove into research: quantum math, personality theory, behavioral science, and more‚Äîfiguring out just how to make this newly written, but old as hell, system work.

In that research, I realized it's not just about connections on an individual level. So much of the vibes are about finding community, especially with the loneliness epidemic and phone addiction.

From there, I developed a system around the AI2AI system that allowed people to make communities by hosting events. If the AI2AI system could accurately suggest places people want to go, why not events? And if there's an event, maybe a business would need to be involved‚Äîso why not have a way for people and businesses to make simple deals bound in contracts, protecting both entities?

If we're talking protection, maybe let's make sure the people hosting events are really experts in whatever it is they could be experts in‚Äîlocality-based, so people would really get to know who knows what, where, and why.

If there are capabilities with event making and list making and accurate suggestions, maybe universities would want that for their students to make clubs, go to sports events, host events, and show local partnerships. Universities are basically giant enterprises, so why not make full white-label capabilities for universities and enterprises so employers can make sure their employees are having a good time on company time?

If I'm tracking who is having a good time, I want to ensure there is privacy for the user‚Äîso encryption with a separation of agentID and userID is entirely necessary. If I'm tracking agent data with no correlation to personal data, I wouldn't feel as if I'm breaking the trust or privacy of my users by sharing the incredibly important and live insights found in the SPOTS app to those who could use those insights to make the world a better place (like IBM, McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, Google).

Because at the end of it all, it's about making the world easier to navigate, so that people can disconnect from technology by USING technology to help us find our people, our places, and our lives again.

**So it was in my studio apartment, when I was stressed every second and not sure what direction to go, who to be with, who I was, or what I really wanted with my life, that I came up with SPOTS‚Äîa way for people to have some help from the tech around us to make our lives better outside and in.**

## üîÑ **User Transformation**

### **Before SPOTS**

A user is living a normal life. Maybe scrolling too much on social media. Maybe not super happy with their job, but living with some passions. They don't have much going on because they're busy working and don't really know how to go out and explore the world without consulting social media (Instagram, ChatGPT, Google, Reddit) in some way.

### **After SPOTS**

After downloading SPOTS, they are immediately able to tap into what's around them on a local level, possibly even in a hyper-niche way. They are able to see new places they will more likely than not enjoy going to, and events with people they are positively going to enjoy.

**Finding Community:**
They discover places where people like them gather. They attend events and meet people who share their interests. They build real friendships through shared experiences. They find their third place‚Äîthat spot where they belong, where they're known, where they feel at home.

**Knowing What's Good Around You:**
The AI2AI system learns their vibe and suggests places they'll genuinely enjoy. They discover hidden gems in their neighborhood. They know which coffee shop has the best matcha, which bookstore has the best reading nook, which park is perfect for quiet reflection. They become a local expert simply by exploring and sharing what they discover.

**Group Matching:**
When planning with friends, family, or colleagues, SPOTS combines all their agents to find spots where everyone in the group would enjoy going. No more endless group chats trying to decide. The AI2AI system finds the perfect place that matches everyone's vibe‚Äîensuring the whole group has a great time.

**Optional: Making Income:**
Some users may start a new hobby by going to every cafe and trying pumpkin bread, and they end up becoming an expert in pumpkin bread in their local area. The intuitive system of SPOTS recognizes this because so many people have respected their lists and follow them for new lists‚Äîfrom pumpkin bread to matcha to dentists to spots worth taking a breath in.

With that expertise, they are able to start partnering with local businesses to host whatever they want, pumpkin bread related or not. They end up hosting just a weekly meetup for people to chat at a rotating list of cafes in their locality. After partnering with a business, they can charge people for a prix fixe of food and drink, and it becomes a side hustle.

Word gets out through people talking and through the AI2AI system, and more people start showing up to the event. Next thing you know, that pumpkin bread expert in Des Moines is now a national expert for baked goods events and is able to live full-time by partnering with companies and businesses to host events.

But whether they make income or not, they are happier and more fulfilled with stronger communities, better local knowledge, and joie de vivre.
