## ‚ö†Ô∏è **WHAT COULD BE BETTER**

### **1. 30-Second Pitch is Too Long**
**Issue:** The 30-second version with all statistics is actually 60-90 seconds.

**Current:** Includes all 5 problems with full statistics
**Problem:** Too much information for 30 seconds

**Recommendation:** Create two versions:
- **30-second (true):** Just the hook and core value prop
- **60-second (enhanced):** Include key statistics

**Example 30-second:**
"SPOTS solves five crises: loneliness, choice paralysis, workforce instability, privacy concerns, and phone addiction. We use AI to match you with places, events, and people you'll love‚Äîall while protecting your privacy completely. Find your people, your places, and your life."

**Priority:** HIGH - 30-second pitch must actually be 30 seconds

---

### **2. Statistics Overload**
**Issue:** Too many statistics can overwhelm and reduce impact.

**Current:** Every problem has 5-7 statistics
**Problem:** Information overload, key stats get lost

**Recommendation:** Use the "Rule of 3":
- Pick the 3 most impactful statistics per problem
- Lead with the most shocking/relatable
- Use others as supporting details

**Example:**
- **Loneliness:** "40% of adults feel lonely. 60% drop in close friendships. Third places disappearing."
- (Supporting: health impacts, young adult stats, etc.)

**Priority:** MEDIUM - Better impact with fewer, stronger stats

---

### **3. Repetitive Content**
**Issue:** Same information repeated across multiple sections.

**Current:** Problems explained in 30-second, 2-minute, and 10-minute versions
**Problem:** Redundancy, longer documents

**Recommendation:** 
- Use cross-references instead of repetition
- "See [section] for details"
- Create modular sections that can be combined

**Priority:** LOW - Minor issue, but improves readability

---

### **4. Visual Outline Inconsistencies**
**Issue:** Visual outline still references "three problems" in some places.

**Current:** Some slides mention 3 problems, some mention 5
